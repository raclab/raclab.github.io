About RACLAB

Robotics, Automation & Control
Laboratory

Founded in 2014 under the leadership of Prof. Dr. Akif DURDU, RACLAB is a project-based R&D laboratory at Konya Technical University where undergraduate and graduate students work alongside academics on real-world engineering challenges.

Our philosophy: students learn not only technical skills but also teamwork, communication, and product-development thinking through real problem scenarios.

Research Areas

What We Work On

RACLAB conducts advanced R&D across six core domains, integrating perception, planning, control, and communication into deployable autonomous systems.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV)

Design and development of fixed-wing & rotary-wing UAV platforms. Autonomous flight, GPS-denied navigation, real-time image processing, swarm coordination, and indigenous flight control software.

SLAM Vision Swarm Deep Learning
Learn More

Autonomous Ground Vehicles (UGV)

Sensor-fusion-based localization, lane detection, obstacle avoidance, path planning and autonomous control for self-driving car platforms and industrial AGVs.

LiDAR ROS SLAM AI Control
Learn More

Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUV)

GNSS-independent navigation using IMU–DVL–camera–depth sensor fusion, obstacle detection/avoidance, and autonomous mission planning for underwater environments.

DVL IMU Fusion Sonar
Learn More

Smart Transportation Systems

IoT-based adaptive traffic signal control, V2I communication modules, digital twin technology for intersections, and AI-driven macroscopic traffic analysis software.

IoT V2I Digital Twin
Learn More

Human-Robot Interaction

Estimating and reshaping human intention in collaborative HRI scenarios using HMM and OOM methods. Gesture recognition, action understanding, and safe motion planning.

HMM OOM Gesture
Learn More

Sensor Fusion & SLAM

Multi-sensor integration (IMU, LiDAR, camera, GPS) for robust state estimation, simultaneous localization and mapping, and multi-agent situational awareness.

LiDAR IMU EKF Visual SLAM
Learn More
